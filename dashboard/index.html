Note on wearable data extraction: Consumer wearables including the Redmi Watch 5 Lite do not expose a live data API. In this proof-of-concept, physiological data (HR, SpO₂, steps) is exported manually from Apple Health after each walk using the Health Auto Export app, then matched to walk sessions by timestamp using the Python script. The production study will use a device with an open API (e.g. Polar H10 or Garmin) to automate this step entirely.

Research Context

Understanding why this pipeline exists and what it will enable at scale.

🔬
What This Pipeline Measures
Three data streams are captured per walk and merged into a single timestamped record:

Automatic during the walk — GPS trajectory (every 10 s), walk duration, session ID, and full pre- and post-walk questionnaire responses (sleep, pain, energy, fatigue, exertion, confidence).

Manual after the walk — physiological data from the wearable (heart rate, SpO₂, steps, active energy) exported from Apple Health and matched to the session by timestamp.

Together these give a richer picture of walking burden than any single instrument can provide alone.
🏙️
Why It Matters for Health & Transport Research
Walking is the foundation of urban mobility, yet most transport models treat pedestrians as homogeneous and assume constant walking speed. For people managing chronic conditions — pain, fatigue, cardiovascular or respiratory disease — the physiological cost of walking varies dramatically by day, route, and health state.

This pipeline enables researchers to quantify that variability objectively, linking where someone walked (GPS route), how hard their body worked (HR, SpO₂), and how they felt (questionnaire) — supporting more inclusive infrastructure design, better health intervention targeting, and evidence-based active travel policy.
🚀
What the Next Phase Will Do at Scale
This proof-of-concept demonstrates the pipeline architecture and validates the data collection workflow. Two limitations are acknowledged and addressed in the next phase:

Wearable API: The production study will use a device with an open real-time API (Polar H10 or Garmin) to eliminate the manual health export step entirely.

Scale: Recruitment will expand to a cohort of participants across diverse health profiles and urban environments, collecting longitudinal data to model how health burden changes with route characteristics (gradient, surface, crowding, air quality).
